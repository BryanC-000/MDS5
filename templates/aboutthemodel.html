Introduction to Models Used

Model 1: InceptionResNetV2 (Our Proposed Model)

InceptionResNetV2 is a convolutional neural network that is trained on more than a million images from the ImageNet database. The network is 164 layers deep and can classify images into 1000 object categories, such as keyboard, mouse, pencil, and many animals. This model that is pre-trained on these object images has been trained with breast cancer histology images in this architecture such that it will be able to classify breast cancer histology images.

Model 2: InceptionV3

The InceptionV3 model is a superior version of the basic mconvolutional neural network Inception V1 which was introduced as GoogLeNet in 2014. It is also pre-trained on the ImageNet database and is 48 layers deep. This pre-trained model has been trained with breast cancer histology images in this architecture such that it will be able to classify breast cancer histology images.

Model 3: ResNet50

ResNet50 is a convolutional neural network that is 50 layers deep. This model is also pre-trained on the ImageNet database and can classify images into 1000 object categories, such as keyboard, mouse, pencil, and many animals. This pre-trained model has been trained with breast cancer histology images in this architecture such that it will be able to classify breast cancer histology images.

How To Interpret Your Results

Benign

A benign tumor is an abnormal but noncancerous collection of cells. If you feel a lump in your breast, your first thought may be that you have breast cancer. Fortunately, a majority of breast lumps are benign, meaning they're not cancerous. Both women and men can develop benign (noncancerous) breast lumps.

In Situ

In Situ is short for In Situ Carcinoma. In Situ Carcinoma is a group of abnormal cells that are found only in the place where they first formed in the body. These abnormal cells may become cancer and spread to nearby normal tissue. In general, carcinoma in situ is the earliest form of cancer, and is considered stage 0 cancer that has not yet spread.

Invasive

Invasive is short for Invasive Carcinoma. Invasive Carcinoma cells are cancerous and this means that these abnormal cells growing in the lining of the milk ducts have changed and invaded breast tissue beyond the walls of the duct.

Normal

This means that your breast cells are normal and healthy, as there were no abnormal cells detected!
